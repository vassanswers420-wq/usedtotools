Understanding Systematic Withdrawal Plans (SWP)

SWP allows investors to withdraw a fixed amount from their investment periodically. It ensures disciplined withdrawals while benefiting from compounding growth on the remaining balance. Proper planning can help meet retirement income needs or regular expenses.

Benefits of Using SWP

  • Regular income without liquidating entire investment.
  • Tax-efficient withdrawals based on fund type.
  • Flexibility to adjust withdrawal amount or duration.
  • Suitable for retirement planning or long-term goals.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. Is SWP better than a Fixed Deposit?

SWP can be more tax-efficient and potentially inflation-protected compared to fixed deposits, depending on fund type and returns.

2. Can the SWP run out of money?

Yes, if monthly withdrawals exceed growth, the capital may deplete before the planned duration.

3. Is SWP suitable for retirement planning?

Yes, SWP is commonly used to generate a steady income stream during retirement.
